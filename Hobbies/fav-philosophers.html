Philosophers

Laozi

Ancient Chinese philosopher and author of the famous treatise "Tao Te Ching". His teaching is based on the idea of Tao, the natural way of the world. He taught to live in harmony with nature, not to fight with the flow of life and to follow the principle of wu-wei - "non-action", when actions occur naturally and without violence to the world. His philosophy greatly influenced Taoism and Eastern thought.

Niccolò Machiavelli

Italian philosopher and political thinker of the Renaissance. He is best known for his book "The Prince", in which he analyzed power, politics and ways to maintain it. Machiavelli viewed politics as the practical art of statecraft and often emphasized that a ruler must be willing to make tough decisions for the sake of the stability and strength of the state.

Socrates

One of the most famous philosophers of Ancient Greece. He did not write books, but his ideas were preserved thanks to his students, especially Plato. Socrates taught the search for truth through dialogue and asking questions – this method is known as Socratic dialogue. His main idea was that a person should strive for self-knowledge and moral wisdom.
